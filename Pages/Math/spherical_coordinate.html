Spherical coordinate.



The spherical coordinate system is a curvillinear orthogonal coordinate system of the Euclidean space, as an alternative to the Cartesian coordiante system, cylindrical coordinate system, etc.

Definition \[\begin{align*} x &= r\sin \theta\cos\phi \ \ \ \ \ \ &r &= \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}\\ y &= r\sin\theta\sin\phi\ \ \ \ \ &\theta &= \arctan(\frac{\sqrt{x^2+y^2}}{z}) \\ z &= r\cos\theta \ \ \ \ \ &\phi &= \arctan(\frac{y}{x}) \end{align*}\] Natural basis \[\bm{r} = \begin{pmatrix} \sin \theta \cos \phi \\ \sin \theta \sin \phi \\ \cos \theta \end{pmatrix}\ \ \bm{{\theta}} = \begin{pmatrix} r\cos \theta \cos \phi \\ r\cos \theta \sin \phi \\ - r\sin \theta \end{pmatrix}\ \ \bm{{\phi}} = \begin{pmatrix} - r\sin\theta\sin \phi \\ r\sin\theta\cos \phi \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}\] Change of basis matrix \[\bm{J} = \begin{pmatrix} \sin \theta \cos \phi & r\cos \theta \cos \phi & - r\sin\theta\sin \phi \\ \sin \theta \sin \phi & r\cos \theta \sin \phi & r\sin\theta\cos \phi \\ \cos \theta & - r\sin \theta & 0 \end{pmatrix}\ \ \]
Example 1. Convert the electric field of a point charge \(q\) in spherical coordinates representation: \[\bm{E}(r,\theta,\phi) = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{r^2}\bm{\hat{r}}\] to a function \(\bm{E}: \mathbb{R}^3 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3\).

Solution. Substitute the scalers and vectors as follows: \[\begin{align*}\bm{E} &= \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{r^2}\bm{\hat{r}} \\ &= \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{r^2}\bm{r} \\&= \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}(\sin \theta \cos \phi \bm{\hat{x}} + \sin \theta \sin \phi \bm{\hat{y}}+ \cos \theta \bm{\hat{z}}) \\&= \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}(\sin (\arctan(\frac{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}}{z})) \cos (\arctan (\frac{y}{x})) \bm{\hat{x}} + \sin (\arctan(\frac{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}}{z})) \sin (\arctan (\frac{y}{x})) \bm{\hat{y}}+ \cos (\arctan(\frac{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}}{z})) \bm{\hat{z}}) \\&= \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}(\frac{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}} \frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 }} \bm{\hat{x}} + \frac{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}} \frac{y}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 }} \bm{\hat{y}}+ \frac{z}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}} \bm{\hat{z}}) \\&= \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}(\frac{x}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}} \bm{\hat{x}} + \frac{y}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}} \bm{\hat{y}}+ \frac{z}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}} \bm{\hat{z}}) \end{align*} \] Therefore, the desired function is defined by \(\bm{E}(x,y,z) = (\frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{x}{(x^2 + y^2 + z^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}}, \frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{y}{(x^2 + y^2 + z^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}}, \frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{z}{(x^2 + y^2 + z^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}})\) Example 2. Verify the result in Ex.1 using the change of basis matrix.

Solution. \[\begin{align*} \begin{pmatrix} E_r\\ E_{\theta} \\ E_{\phi}\end{pmatrix} &= \begin{pmatrix} \sin \theta \cos \phi & r\cos \theta \cos \phi & - r\sin\theta\sin \phi \\ \sin \theta \sin \phi & r\cos \theta \sin \phi & r\sin\theta\cos \phi \\ \cos \theta & - r\sin \theta & 0 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{x}{(x^2 + y^2 + z^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}}\\ \frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{y}{(x^2 + y^2 + z^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}} \\ \frac{q}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{z}{(x^2 + y^2 + z^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}})\end{pmatrix}\\ &= \begin{pmatrix} \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q}{r^2}\\ 0\\ 0\end{pmatrix} \end{align*} \] The two examples give consistent results.
