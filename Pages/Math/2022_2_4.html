Finding a functional's extrema with du Bois Reymond's lemma.



Find all possible extrema of \(J: C[0, \frac{\pi}{6}] \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\), where \(J\) is defined by \(J(y) = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6}sec^2 y'^3\).

\(Solution.\)
First find admissible directions for \(J\), which satisfy the following for some small \(\epsilon \neq 0\): \[y + \epsilon v \in \bold{D}\] Which means:\[ \begin{align*} (y + \epsilon v)(0) &= 1\\ v(0)& = 0 \end{align*}\] Also:\[ \begin{align*} (y + \epsilon v)(\frac{\pi}{6}) &= \frac{3}{2}\\ v(\frac{\pi}{6}) &= 0 \\ \end{align*}\] Note the above two identities are used as conditions for using \(du\ Bois\ Reymond's\ lemma\) later. Next we calculate the derivative.\\\\ First we recognize: \[f(x,y,z) = \sec^2(x) z^3\] So: \[f_y(x,y,z) = 0,\ f_z(x,y,z) = 3\sec^2(x)z^2\] Calculate:\[ \begin{align*} \delta J(y;v) & = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} [f_y(x,y,y') \cdot v + f_z(x,y,y') \cdot v']\\ & =3\int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \sec^2 y'^2 v'\\ \end{align*}\] At a local extremum: \[\int_0^\frac{\pi}{6} \sec^2 y'^2 v'=0\] Using \(du\ Bois\ Reymond's\ lemma\): \[\sec^2 y'^2\ is\ constant.\] Let that constant be \(C\). In the following, \(A,\ B\) are also constants:\[ \begin{align*} \sec^2 y'^2 &= C \\ y'(x) &= \sqrt{\frac{C}{\sec^2(x)}} \\ y'(x) &= \sqrt{C} \frac{1}{\sec(x)} \\ y(x) &= A \sin(x) +B\\ \end{align*}\] Therefore, all possible extremas are in the set: \[\{y: \exists a \in \mathbb{R},\ \exists b \in \mathbb{R},\ \forall x \in [a,b]: y(x) = a \sin(x) +b\}\] Feb 4th, 2022
