Continuity.



We say a function \(f: \mathbf{X} \rightarrow \mathbf{Y}\) is continuous at \(x_0\) iff \[\lim_{x\rightarrow x_0} f(x) = f(x_0)\] which is equivalent to this alternative formulation: \[\forall a_n \neq x_0 \in \mathbf{X}: \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} a_n = x_0 \Rightarrow \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} f(a_n) \rightarrow f(x_0)\]
Here are various types of continuity:

\(f: \mathbf{X} \rightarrow \mathbf{Y}\) is said to be continuous iff \[\forall x_0 \in X, \forall \epsilon > 0, \exists \delta > 0, \forall x \in X: d(x,x_0) < \delta \Rightarrow d(f(x), f(x_0)) < \epsilon\] \(f: \mathbf{X} \rightarrow \mathbf{Y}\) is said to be uniformly continuous iff \[\forall \epsilon > 0, \exists \delta > 0, \forall x_0 \in X, \forall x \in X: d(x,x_0) < \delta \Rightarrow d(f(x), f(x_0)) < \epsilon\] \(f: \mathbf{X} \rightarrow \mathbf{Y}\) is said to be lipshitz continuous iff \[\exists k > 0, \forall x_1, x_2 \in \mathbf{X}: d(x_1,x_2) < k \cdot d(f(x_1), f(x_2))\] \(f: \mathbf{X} \rightarrow \mathbf{Y}\) is said to be α-Hölder continuous iff \[\exists k > 0, \forall x_1, x_2 \in \mathbf{X}: d(x_1,x_2)^\alpha < k \cdot d(f(x_1), f(x_2))\] Created Mar 28th, 2022.
