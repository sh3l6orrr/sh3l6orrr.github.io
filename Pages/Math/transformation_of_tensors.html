Transformation of tensors.



Definition

For a contravariant tensor field of order 1, \(A^i\), defined in a coordinate system \((x^i)\), the contravariant transformation law to a coordinate system \((x'^i)\), where it has components \(A'^i\), is given by: \[A'^i = \frac{\partial x'^i}{\partial x^j} A^j\] A similar formula holds for a contravariant tensor field of order 2, and more: \[A'^{ij} = \frac{\partial x'^i}{\partial x^l} \frac{\partial x'^j}{\partial x^m}A^{lm}\] Covariant derivatives of tensors are also tensors, and hence satisfy this tranformation law as well: \[A'^i_{;k} = \frac{\partial x'^i}{\partial x^j}\frac{\partial x'^l}{\partial x^k} A^j_{;l}\]
Example 1. The spherical coordinate system is given by: \[\begin{align*} x &= r\sin \theta\cos\phi\\ y &= r\sin\theta\sin\phi\\ z &= r\cos\theta \end{align*}\] Convert the following tensor field in spherical coordinates to Cartesian coordinates. \[\bm{A}(r,\theta,\phi) = r \bm{e}_r\]
Solution. Notice \(A^r = r\), while \(A^{\theta} = A^{\phi} = 0\). Then: \[A'^x = \frac{\partial x'^x}{\partial x^r} A^r + \frac{\partial x'^x}{\partial x^{\theta}} A^{\theta} + \frac{\partial x'^x}{\partial x^{\phi}} A^{\phi} = r\sin \theta\cos\phi = x\] Similarly, \(A'^y = y\) and \(A'^z = z\). Therefore, this tensor field is transformed to: \[\bm{A}(x,y,z) = x \bm{i}+ y \bm{j} + z \bm{k}\]
