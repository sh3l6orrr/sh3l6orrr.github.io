Compactness.



We say a set is compact iff it's complete and total-bounded.

Since complete sets are closed and total-bounded sets are bounded, compact sets are also closed and bouneded. The converse is not true.

In Euclidean space, however, the converse is true. by Heine-Borel Theorem, a closed and bounded set in Euclidean space is compact. By Bolzano-Weierstrass Theorem, a closed and bounded set in Euclidean space is sequentially compact, too.
